\section{Project Overview}

READCRAFTERY is an educational word puzzle game for children ages 5--9, built in Godot 4.
The game teaches reading through interactive puzzles set in a magical library world.
Players read short passages, then search for target words guided by their companion
Owlorumo --- a young owl wizard whose power returns as the child reads.

The visual style is pixel art with a warm, magical, child-friendly aesthetic:
\textbf{Stardew Valley} meets a \textbf{1980s illustrated storybook}.

\subsection{Target Platforms}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=2pt]
  \item HTML5 (itch.io) --- primary
  \item Android / iOS
  \item PC (Windows / Linux / macOS)
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Art Style --- Storybook Arcane}

The style has a name: \textbf{Storybook Arcane}. Key principles:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=2pt]
  \item Pixel art with deliberate organic shapes --- curves, not sharp geometry
  \item Warm candlelight as primary light source --- upper left in all scenes
  \item Magic is ambient, not explosive --- soft glows, floating particles, pulsing runes
  \item Textures are invented --- textures that only exist in this world
  \item Characters have exaggerated expressiveness --- big eyes, never frightening
  \item No dark fantasy --- no skulls, no scary darkness; the wizard is always kind
\end{itemize}

\subsection{The Library Through Restoration}

The same place is encountered under three progressively revealed names:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{The Sleeping Library} --- the first state; dormant, veiled, waiting
  \item \textbf{The Reawakened Library} --- a warmer, more responsive state after meaningful restoration
  \item \textbf{The Library of Beginnings} --- the deeper revealed truth; restoration does not return a pristine past, it opens new continuity
\end{itemize}

These are not separate worlds or separate levels. They are names for the same living library-world as it becomes more present.

\subsection{Master Color Palette}

All assets must use \textbf{only} these colors. No additional colors without approval.

\vspace{6pt}
\colorswatch{browncol}{2C1A0E}{background --- main background, dark warm brown}

  The accent gold (\hex{D4A017}) is sacred --- do not substitute with other yellows.
  Gold should feel meaningful and warm, but never like score or trophy UI.
\end{warnbox}

\section{Owlorumo --- Character Design}

\subsection{The Story (context for the artist)}

\begin{storybox}
{\sffamily\small\color{storytext}
Owlorumo was the companion owl of Athena. As the habit of reading dimmed in the human world,
his memory, power, and presence diminished with it. He remains in the library --- the last
place where wisdom survived long enough to be reawakened.

\vspace{6pt}
The child is never told this directly. Owlorumo does not explain it. But each recognized word,
pattern, and finished passage returns a little more light to what was fading.

\vspace{6pt}
\textbf{Reading is a power that discovers and restores human knowledge.}

\vspace{6pt}
\textit{This story lives in visual details: the staff crystal, Owlorumo's eyes, the warmth of
his presence, and the awakening of the library itself. \textbf{state\_4} is a deliberate homage
to Tolkien's Gandalf the White: fulfilled, not replaced. The blue mantle remains visible.
The moon brooch, belt, boots, and organic staff form remain. The transformation is one of
light, material, and presence. Never explained. Only discovered.}
}
\end{storybox}

\subsection{Personality}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=2pt]
  \item Young, curious, slightly clumsy with magic but never with words
  \item Companion, not authority --- discovers alongside the child
  \item Amber eyes are his most expressive feature: wide with wonder, warm with encouragement
  \item Never intimidating, never scared, never disappointed
  \item No beard --- Owlorumo is young
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Character Anatomy}

\subsubsection{Proportions}
Compact and slightly chubby --- reads as young. Large round head. \textbf{No beard.}

\subsubsection{Clothing --- confirmed from concept art}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=2pt]
  \item Navy blue robe --- deep, dark (\hex{1E2A4A}). Slightly oversized (apprentice feel)
  \item Gold trim on robe hem and cuffs (\hex{D4A017})
  \item Neck wrap --- same navy fabric, loosely wrapped
  \item \textbf{Crescent moon brooch} --- \hex{D4A017}. Always present. Identity detail.
  \item Brown leather belt with gold buckle
  \item Tall pointed wizard hat --- same navy, slightly bent. Feather + rune engravings.
  \item Short brown leather boots --- slightly oversized, covers talons completely
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Staff --- Integral Element (NEVER remove)}

The staff is a \textbf{permanent} part of Owlorumo's design --- present in every frame.

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=2pt]
  \item Dark twisted organic wood --- darker than the robe
  \item Purple crystal on top --- \hex{7B4FBE} in idle, brighter in active states
  \item Owlorumo holds the staff in his \textbf{LEFT hand}, reaching above his head
  \item The crystal glow is the single most recognizable silhouette element after the hat
\end{itemize}

\begin{notebox}
  Owlorumo's silhouette must always read as ``owl with wizard hat and staff'' at 64x64px.
  If the staff cannot fit legibly, show the crystal glow at minimum.
\end{notebox}

\subsection{Eyes}

At 64x64px the eyes will be 4--5 pixels wide each. Every emotion comes through the eyes.

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=2pt]
  \item Bright amber-gold (\hex{D4A017} tint toward orange)
  \item Large and round --- Seuss-style, not realistic
  \item Independent eyebrows (owls don't have them --- intentional)
  \item Slow blink in idle = signal of trust and warmth
  \item \textbf{Each eye has a 1-pixel white highlight in the upper-left corner.}
        This is what makes them read as alive. Do not omit.
\end{itemize}

  \textbf{Crystal behavioral states — animation guidance:}\\[4pt]
  The crystal communicates four distinct states through animation:
  \begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=2pt]
    \item \textbf{Idle pulse} — soft \hex{7B4FBE} pulse at 0.8Hz. Base state.
    \item \textbf{Hint active} — brightens to \hex{A87FE8}, staff points toward puzzle area.
    \item \textbf{Fragment absorption} — brief white-gold flash when a puzzle is completed.
      The flash arrives, pulses once, is absorbed. Duration: under 1 second.
      Visually distinct from the idle pulse — brighter, warmer, directional.
    \item \textbf{State base level} — permanent ambient glow determined by owlorumo\_state.
      This is the resting brightness between all other states.
      Fragments arrive on top of this level — they do not replace it.
  \end{itemize}
  The artist must design the crystal so all four states are readable
  at 64×64px native size.
\end{notebox}
\begin{notebox}
  \textbf{state\_0 and state\_1 are M2 blockers} --- deliver these first.\\[4pt]
  \textbf{state\_4} is a homage to Tolkien's Gandalf the White. Never explained in the game.
  Only discovered. The crystal in state\_4 transitions from \hex{7B4FBE} to a white-gold glow
  --- the only state where the crystal color changes.
\end{notebox}

\subsection{Owlorumo in the Library — Ambient Behavior}

Owlorumo is not static in the library. He inhabits the space.
The artist must design animations that communicate this.

\begin{notebox}
  \textbf{Core rule:} Owlorumo is always doing something when the child arrives.
  He finishes his current gesture naturally, then looks at the child.
  He was not waiting. He was here. There is a difference.
\end{notebox}

\textbf{Ambient activities — same action, different energy per state:}

\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{@{}l >{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X >{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X@{}}
  \toprule
  \rowcolor{tabhead}

  \midrule
  \rowcolor{tabrow}Sorting books with magic & Slow, a book falls, he picks it up & Fluid, effortless \\
  Walk to window & Heavy, slightly hunched & Light, curious \\
  \rowcolor{tabrow}Reading at lectern & Eyes moving slowly, sometimes stops & Engaged, occasional soft smile \\
  Writing on parchment & Only state\_2+ & Present from state\_2 \\
  \bottomrule
\end{tabularx}

\vspace{6pt}
\textbf{On child entry:} Owlorumo always notices. He finishes his gesture,
then turns toward the child. Never startled. Never ignoring.

\textbf{On book selection:} a small gesture of recognition —
not celebration, not instruction. As if the child chose exactly right.

\textbf{On tap in library (no active event):} crystal pulses softly,
a glance toward the child. Presence, not function.

\section{Technical Specifications}

\subsection{Pixel Art Rules}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{No anti-aliasing} --- hard pixel edges only
  \item Outline: 1px dark outline using shadow variant of each base color.
        \textbf{Never pure black \#000000}
  \item Light source: always upper left for all assets
  \item Max 20 colors per character sprite (all animation frames combined)
  \item Max 32 colors per background layer
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Owlorumo --- Display Rules}

\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{@{}l c c >{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X@{}}
  \toprule
  \rowcolor{tabhead}
  \color{white}\sffamily\bfseries Context &
  \color{white}\sffamily\bfseries Master &
  \color{white}\sffamily\bfseries Display &
  \color{white}\sffamily\bfseries Scale \\
  \midrule
  \rowcolor{tabrow}PassageView (gameplay) & 64x64 & 192x192 & 3x integer \\
  Library / menu & 64x64 & 256x256 & 4x integer \\
  \rowcolor{tabrow}Celebration / reward & 64x64 & 256--320+ & 4--5x integer \\
  \bottomrule

\begin{notebox}
  Both 3x and 4x are integer scales from 64px. Godot renders pixel-perfect with
  \texttt{texture\_filter = NEAREST}. \textbf{One atlas per state, multiple display contexts.}
\end{notebox}

\subsection{Atlas Delivery Format}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=2pt]
  \item 5 PNG files: \texttt{atlas\_owlorumo\_state0.png} through \texttt{state4.png}
  \item Identical frame layout across all 5 atlases --- transparent background
  \item Frames left-to-right in rows, consistent grid, no padding
  \item Atlas max size: 2048x2048px
  \item Anchor point: center-bottom (character feet)
  \item Deliver Aseprite \texttt{.ase} source + flattened PNG
\end{itemize}
